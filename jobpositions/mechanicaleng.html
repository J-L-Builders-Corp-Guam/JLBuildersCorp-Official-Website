Mechanical Engineering

Job Summary:

The Mechanical Engineer position is responsible for overseeing the installation, repair, and maintenance of centralized heat, water, gas and other related systems, as well planning and designing tools, engines, and other related machines.

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
  • Plans, designs, and develops mechanical system of the construction projects, such as cooling, heating, and plumbing using local, state, and company engineering standards.
  • Analyzes and interprets blueprints, technical drawing, load data sheets, and equipment specification using computer aided design (CAD) and computer aided engineering (CAE) software.
  • Works in conjunction with the Company’s Mechanical Engineering Team in preparing, assessing constructability, and applying value engineering methodologies to the preliminary design drawings and specifications for the mechanical systems, components, and equipment for the prospective Design Build Project.
  • Ensures the appropriate and required testing program is implemented with local, state, and company engineering standards in fire protection, HVAC and plumbing.
  • Confers with foreman, sub-contractors, inspectors, and suppliers to determine project details, such as plan preparation, tools and materials, acceptance testing, evaluation of field conditions and coordination of various phases of construction relative to mechanical scope of work.
  • Coordinates with procurement with regards to material requisitions and purchased orders.
  • Prepares reports, determines construction phase schedules, and calculates cost estimates.
  • Inspects mechanical sites to monitor progress and direct work by construction superintendent, construction workforce, and subcontractors in performing the mechanical works to ensure conformance to mechanical engineering plans and specification and construction and safety standards.
  • Creates and implements instructions necessary for systems to function, such as maintenance policies and procedures.
  • Reviews construction drawings and makes the design changes as necessary to suit the actual site condition and as required by the client.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.
Required Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:
  • Demonstrates effective and proficient skills in both oral and written communication.
  • Possesses organizational skills and attention to detail.
  • Skilled in active listening, retaining information, and asking questions as appropriate.
  • Has effective teamwork and collaborative skills.
  • Has the ability to perform technological duties such as using computers or other computer systems to perform data entering, creating purchasing orders, and more.
  • Has the ability to analyze information and effectively differentiate the pros and cons especially when making decisions.
  • Knowledge of data processing and electronic mail software: Microsoft Excel, Microsoft Word, Microsoft Outlook, etc.
  • Knowledge of electrical manufacturing processes.
  • Knowledge of raw materials, production, and other related processes.
Education and Experience:

The Mechanical Engineer position requires a minimum of a bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering and with at least two to five (2-5) years of experience as MEPFS Construction (Mechanical/Electrical/Plumbing/Firepro/Sanitary), Field Engineer (other relatedexperience like consultancy, QA/QC, Estimating, Quantity Surveying, Designing, Piping, Power Plant, Onshore, Offshore, Oil & Gas may NOT be considered). Additionally, having experience in Federal Construction Contract is a plus.
